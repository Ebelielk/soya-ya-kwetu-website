About Soya ya Kwetu

At Soya ya Kwetu, we are deeply rooted in the agri-food sector, committed to transforming lives through healthy and affordable nutrition. Our journey begins in the fertile lands of Kolwezi, specifically Musonoi and Mutoshi, where we cultivate premium soya beans.

These beans are then meticulously processed into high-quality soya powder at our small mealing factory in Gecamine. Our product stands as a superior alternative to traditional milk powder, offering numerous health benefits—it's heart-friendly, rich in essential vitamins, and entirely 100% bio.

Starting in Kolwezi, our ambition is to expand across the entire Democratic Republic of Congo, providing a vital solution to the prevalent scarcity and high cost of milk, while championing a healthier, natural dietary option for all.

Our Vision

To become the leading distributional channel of soya within the DRC, actively fighting child malnutrition and promoting public health. We aspire to be the symbol of healthy, accessible food, proudly made in Congo, transforming national nutrition sustainably within the next 8 years.

Our Mission

To provide healthy, energetic, and lactose-free food to the population of DRC, emphasizing nutritionally rich soya-based alternatives. Our objective is to significantly reduce child malnutrition in neglected areas and offer a miraculous bio-food solution made in DR Congo.

The Problem We Are Solving

Addressing Malnutrition in DRC

The Democratic Republic of Congo faces significant challenges with malnutrition, particularly in central provinces where access to affordable, nutritious food is limited. Statistics show that over 48% of Congolese children suffer from malnutrition, largely due to the high cost and inaccessibility of powdered milk.

Our solution harnesses the potential of Congo's own soil, utilizing soya—a highly energetic and cost-effective alternative to imported cow's milk. Soya ya Kwetu offers a natural, sustainable, and economically viable option to combat this critical issue.

Beyond Malnutrition: Health & Accessibility

For those who can afford milk, concerns about lactose, cholesterol, and the need for diverse health benefits persist. Soya ya Kwetu provides a miraculous solution: it's naturally free from lactose and cholesterol, packed with health advantages, and serves as an effective food complement for overall well-being.

Furthermore, in regions like Eastern DRC (Goma and Bukavu) affected by conflict, food access is a constant struggle. Soya, being easy to prepare and stock, offers a crucial source of energy and nourishment for vulnerable populations, including prisoners where nutritional situations are often dire.

Who We Serve

Soya ya Kwetu is dedicated to reaching diverse segments of the population, ensuring healthy nutrition is accessible to all.

Supermarkets & Families

We supply major supermarkets like Kin-Market, Katanga Supermarket, and Jambo Market with our packaged soya powder (100g, 200g, 500g), catering to families who prioritize healthy food.

Big Organizations

Partnering with the Congolese government.

Our Impact & Goals

Soya ya Kwetu is driven by ambitious SMART goals, aiming for sustainable growth and significant positive impact across the DRC.

Short-Term Goals

  • Produce 100 kg of soya monthly from Musonoi farm (Feb 1 - June 1).
  • Sell 1,000 packages of soya powder in Katanga Supermarket, Kolwezi within one month:
    • 300 x 100g packages (10,000 CDF unit price)
    • 300 x 200g packages (18,000 CDF unit price)
    • 400 x 500g packages (40,000 CDF unit price)

Medium-Term Goals

  • Expand soya business to Lubumbashi and Kalemi for partnership with Kin-Market by January 2027.
  • Produce 800 kg of soya monthly from Musonoi and Mutshatsha farms (Feb 1 - June 1, 2026).
  • Sell 14,000 packages of soya powder in Kolwezi and Lubumbashi within a year (2028):
    • 3,500 x 100g packages
    • 3,500 x 200g packages
    • 6,500 x 500g packages

Long-Term Goals

  • Establish 4 new soya fields (at least 20 hectares each) in Kinshasa, Lubumbashi, Bukavu, Goma, and Kisangani by end of 2030.
  • Sign partnership with Congolese government to be 20% soya provider for central prisons in Kinshasa, Kolwezi, Lubumbashi, Goma, and Bukavu by end of December 2030.
  • Reintegrate 20 prisoners from Kolwezi into life after prison by offering jobs in Musonoi and Mutoshi soya fields (December 2030).
